Landline Phone

A landline phone, also known as a fixed-line telephone, is a communication device that connects to a telephone network via physical wires or cables. Unlike mobile phones, which rely on wireless transmission, landline phones typically have a stationary base unit connected to a wall outlet, with a handset that can be picked up to make or receive calls.

They provide reliable voice communication over dedicated lines and are often associated with residential and business environments. While their usage has declined with the advent of mobile and internet-based communication technologies, landline phones still play a role in many households and businesses, offering clear audio quality and dependable service.
